My name is Louiza. I'm a Master's student in Product Development at the University of Antwerp. Ever since I was little, I've been fascinated by beauty — from museums on holiday to the little things in everyday life. Later on, I attended art school in my spare time. From pen and paper to altering old clothes. Making things, repairing them or creating them myself, always on the lookout for new ways to keep my hands busy.

Why is something made in a certain way? How do the parts fit together? What is it made of? How does it work? These questions often go round and round in my head. When the product development course was suggested to me, I no longer had to think about what I would do after secondary school. Now, after three years of hard work, I'm still happy with my choice and couldn't imagine doing anything else. It feels as though I can utilise and challenge all my talents here.

I love all the aspects of the design process that involve aesthetics, coming up with ideas, and physically creating and visualising them. Through mood boards, I'm discovering new styles, and new challenges don't put me off. I'm passionate about the subject and dream of being able to share this with others and make a contribution. Take a look here at the start of my creative journey in search of functional beauty.

.01

Paper
IKEA Lamp

Individual Project
December 2021
6 Weeks
Tools — Design, Prototyping, SolidWorks, Laser Cutting, Adobe InDesign
Brief

Throughout the course, we are reminded with every project to design within the circular economy. This lamp was our first encounter with this concept. Favini introduced us to their Crush paper collection — papers made from the residue of citrus fruits, grapes, maize and more. They asked us to design a lamp using paper from the Crush range that would fit onto a standard IKEA light fitting.

Process

The design process began with the creation of a physical collage, to draw inspiration for the lamp's shapes. Using the shapes and contrasts from that collage, the limits of paper were explored through sketching and folding studies before settling on a final form.

The cut-out sheet can be sold alongside a matching IKEA light fitting. According to the included instructions, the lamp can be easily assembled by the buyer themselves — no tools required.

Memoria

Group Project
December 2021
6 Weeks
Tools — Sketching, Prototyping, SolidWorks, Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, After Effects
In collaboration with — Kobe Hallans
First Prototype

In this first flexible prototype for UX testing, we created a model that offers the same design possibilities as the final product. It can be worn around the neck or wrapped around the wrist. With this foam model, each wearing style could already be analysed during the first round of user testing.

Refining the Design

Due to changes made to the design, it was no longer necessary to maintain the same level of flexibility as in the first prototype. The refined foam model was slightly flexible, making it easy to hang around the neck and connect for activation. The various cut-outs were incorporated in detail, just as they would be in the final concept.

In collaboration with Kobe Hallans

Brace

Group Project
May 2024
7 Weeks
Tools — Sketching, Procreate, Prototyping, SolidWorks, Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign
In collaboration with — Roosmarie Mennes, Daphne Mulkers, Kauan Steurs
Brief

Starting from inclusive design, we considered the stigmas surrounding medical devices in everyday life. Wearables where functionality takes precedence over aesthetics result in an unusual object that makes the wearer stand out from their surroundings. We were commissioned to design a stigma-free aid for a specific target group — taking an existing design to the next level by transforming a medical necessity into an attractive product that users actually want to wear.

NEX — the brace with mouse function

Carpal tunnel syndrome causes pain and numbness in people who spend a lot of time working at a computer. Although a brace helps, for many people taking that step is a big one. That's why we designed NEX: a stigma-free, functional hand brace that combines comfort and technology.

Thanks to a built-in computer mouse in the palm, ergonomically sound materials and a breathable design, the wearer can switch effortlessly between typing and using the mouse — an aesthetic aid that fits seamlessly into the office environment.

NEX, the brace with mouse function. A clever and handy medical aid that fits seamlessly into the office environment.
In collaboration with Roosmarie Mennes, Daphne Mulkers, Kauan Steurs

.04

Compost
— Toilet

Individual Project
May 2024
8 Weeks
Tools — Prototyping, Procreate, SolidWorks, SolidWorks Visualize, Adobe Photoshop, InDesign
Brief

Design a composting or dry toilet for the broader events sector and beyond. Developed in collaboration with WeComeAsFriends, the project addresses the well-known discomfort of temporary toilets at festivals, translating it into a more climate-neutral solution — one that sets Belgium, a country known for its fantastic festivals, apart from the rest.

Chemical toilets, the usual default at festivals and events, are harmful to the environment and waste a lot of water. As a third-year Bachelor's student in Product Development, designing a real alternative wasn't just a vague idea but a concrete goal.

Urine Separation

Due to the lack of sawdust, odour has to be prevented by other means. Urine separation is one such method. I used two different systems which, together, ensure optimal urine separation: a urine trap directs the largest quantities of liquid straight to the liquid compartment, while small holes in the base of the solids compartment let any liquid that missed the trap seep through. A layer of jute over the holes ensures that only liquid can pass.

Airflow & odour control — tests using a scale model showed that a constant airflow, achieved by combining a chimney extraction system with ventilation holes beneath the door and in the stairwells, is the best solution for minimising odour nuisance.
Logistics & maintenance — the cabins are delivered ready-to-use and can be installed by a single person using a forklift. Sealable waste containers are designed to handle 120 people per day and can be moved with a pallet truck, easing the workload on the maintenance team — after the event, the distributor collects everything from a central location.

.05

Devine Dining
with TABA

Group Project
May 2025
8 Weeks
Tools — Prototyping, SolidWorks, SolidWorks Visualize, Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, After Effects
In collaboration with — Kato Van Grimbergen, Evert Vantongerloo, Mijntje Jacobs
The Experience

The menu appears subtly, with blurred dishes becoming sharp as two light lines descend. These lines guide a rising relief, pressing against the fabric as tabs unfold. When a tab is pressed, the lights close in and capture the selection — the line moves downward, the top LED dims, and the bottom LED glows. This repeats for each course.

After all choices are made, everything fades. Only the "U" in MENU remains — then the course you chose lights up. The final motion pulls the last light to the bottom, leaving the table in a soft glow.

TABA — the Brand

The project's branding and design pay homage to Japanese simplicity, expressed through clean lines, intuitive motion and a quiet presence. To reflect this harmony between culture and design, the logo is inspired by the traditional Japanese Hanko stamp. By hand-carving a woodblock print for the final logo, the inspiration could be carried through seamlessly — from concept to the object on the table.
